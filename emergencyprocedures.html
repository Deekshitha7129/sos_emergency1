Emergency Procedures for Natural Calamities

Drop, Cover, and Hold On:

Drop to your hands and knees, cover your head and neck (or your whole body if possible) under a sturdy piece of furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.

If Indoors:

  • Stay inside. Move away from windows, glass, and heavy objects that could fall.
  • If no furniture is available, sit against an interior wall and protect your head and neck.

If Outdoors:

  • Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
  • Drop to the ground to avoid being knocked over.

If Driving:

  • Pull over safely and stay inside the vehicle until the shaking stops.
  • Avoid stopping on bridges or underpasses.

If You Live in a Landslide-Prone Area:

  • Familiarize yourself with local evacuation routes and signs of potential landslides.

During a Landslide:

  • Move quickly to higher ground if you can do so safely.
  • If you cannot evacuate, seek shelter in a small, sturdy building or vehicle if available.

Post-Landslide:

  • Avoid entering areas where landslides have occurred until they are declared safe.
  • Report any blocked roads or damage to authorities.

Before a Fire:

  • Create a defensible space around your home by clearing flammable vegetation.
  • Prepare an evacuation plan and emergency kit with essentials.

During a Fire:

  • Follow evacuation orders promptly.
  • If you are trapped, call 911 and try to find a body of water or an open area with little vegetation.

After a Fire:

  • Stay away from burned areas due to potential structural damage and hazardous materials.
  • Monitor air quality and avoid breathing in smoke.

Before a Flood:

  • Know if you live in a flood-prone area and have an emergency plan and kit ready.
  • Elevate electrical appliances and utilities.

During a Flood:

  • Move to higher ground or an upper floor if you can.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.

After a Flood:

  • Avoid returning to flooded areas until they are declared safe.
  • Clean and disinfect your home and check for structural damage.

Before a Hurricane:

  • Prepare an emergency kit with water, food, and important documents.
  • Secure your home, board up windows, and check the status of storm shelters.

During a Hurricane:

  • Follow evacuation orders and stay indoors away from windows and doors.
  • If you are in a storm surge area, move to higher ground.

After a Hurricane:

  • Avoid downed power lines and flooded areas.
  • Check for gas leaks and report any damage to authorities.

Before a Tornado:

  • Have a plan to move to a small, windowless interior room on the lowest floor of your home.
  • Keep a weather radio handy.

During a Tornado:

  • Seek shelter immediately.
  • Avoid windows and stay in a low-lying area or ditch if you are outdoors and cannot find shelter.

After a Tornado:

  • Stay alert for additional tornadoes.
  • Avoid entering damaged buildings until they are declared safe and check for gas leaks.
